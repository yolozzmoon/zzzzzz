《澳洲大地的心脏》Australia’s Hidden Islands (2020)

Drone and underwater lenses glide across 7,000 forgotten isles, revealing scarlet volcanic reefs, alabaster dunes and jade lagoons. Seabirds thread cyclones; corals spawn under moonlight. The film is a love letter to the South Pacific’s last uncharted blue utopia.

《可可西里》Kekexili: Mountain Patrol (2004)

At 5,000 m, every breath is a gamble. Volunteer patrolmen chase poachers across –40 °C wasteland while protecting the near-mythic Tibetan antelope. Lu Chuan’s camera frames both fallen carcasses and the fragile humanity that guards them.

《澳洲乱世情》Australia (2008)

1940s Northern Territory: British aristocrat Sarah trades ballroom gowns for cattle dust. Alongside drover “Drover,” she drives 1,500 head through firestorms to Darwin, while an Aboriginal boy sings ancient songs above the rumble of Pearl Harbor. A widescreen birth-of-a-nation saga.

《霸王别姬》 Farewell My Concubine (1993)

Spanning warlords, occupation and Cultural Revolution, Peking-opera star Dieyi bleeds his role as Concubine Yu into real life. Betrayed by stage brother Duan Xiaolou and history alike, his final sword becomes an epitaph for beauty crushed by ideology.

《沿江而上》Up the Yangtze (2007)

As the Three Gorges Dam rises one metre daily, a peasant teen serves canapés to tourists to fund her family’s eviction, while an old boatman sings river ballads below deck. The lens floats like the water itself—calm, inevitable, irreversible.

《地球的某个角落：澳洲》Somewhere on Earth: Australia (2019)

Abandoned mining towns, salt-crusted lakes and wind-scoured rail tracks become pages of a geological chronicle. Long 8K aerial pauses hover over cracked riverbeds as if waiting for 40,000-year-old Aboriginal rock art to speak again.

Blue (2017)

From Indonesia’s plastic gyres to Antarctic krill swarms, the film dissects a wounded ocean—90 % of big fish gone, 46,000 micro-plastics per km². Yet it also frames a healed turtle’s first stroke, stubbornly extending a 300-million-year lineage.

《我们诞生在中国》Born in China (2016)

A giant-panda mum nudges her cub off a Sichuan bamboo trunk; a snow leopard teaches her kittens to hunt on the Qilian peaks; a golden-snub-nosed monkey takes his first leap above the clouds. Through family eyes, Lu Chuan captures the tenderness and brutality of China’s high-altitude web of life.
